Things To Do In Finland

Sleep Under the Northern Lights

Finland Is A Land Of Stark Contrasts. In The Summer Months, Hence The Nickname “The Land Of The Midnight Sun.”

Visit A Lighthouse Island

Finland Has The Largest Archipelago In The World. Where There Are Islands, There Are Lighthouses.

Stroll Around An Old Wooden Town

In The Past, All Of Finland's Houses Were Built Of Wood And Usually Painted Red, With A Few Blue And Yellow Thrown In.

Hike A National Park

There Are Over 40 National Parks Scattered Around The Country's Islands, Peat Lands And Fells Offering Pristine Nature And Plenty Of Recreational Activities.

See Iconic Architecture And Designs

Discover The Ultimate Active Adventure Through Finland! From Adrenaline-Fuelled Experiences To Mountain Playgrounds.

Visit A Public Sauna

Finland's National Pastime Is Going To The Sauna - Some People Go Every Day. The Reason For The Finnish Love Of Sauna? Relaxing In Darkness, Often In Silence, Cleanses The Mind And Body.

Meet the real Santa

Many people know Santa comes from Finland. What some don't know, however, is that it's possible to meet him in person all year. Yes, Santa’s official office, which is located in Rovaniemi in the Arctic Circle, is open every day!

Breathe the world's cleanest air

One of the best things in all of Finland is invisible, abundant and totally free. Just take a deep breath! In all seriousness, the air quality in Finland is the best in the world according to data from the World Health Organisation (WHO).
